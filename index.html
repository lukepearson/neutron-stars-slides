Neutron stars

What are they?

The compressed remnants of giant stars

What is left after a supernova

Giant atom cores without electron shells

  • Contains between 1-3 solar masses
  • Compressed into a diameter of only 12-30km
  • Surface temperature ~170 * hotter than the sun
Image: the size of a neutron star compared with the UK
If you fell from a height of 1 meter on a neutron star, you would hit the ground at ~1,500,000 m/s
A piece of neutron star the size of a grain of sand would have a mass of about 29,250,000kg

How are they made?

Neutron stars are formed when the gravitational force exceeds the electron degeneracy pressure

The electron shell can no longer resist the force of gravity, and collapses into the nucleus

Electrons fuse with the protons to make an atomic nucleus of neutrons

Neutron degeneracy pressure prevents them collapsing even further, into black holes

Neutron star ingredients:

  • Get a large star (8-29 solar masses)
  • Cook for ~10 million years, or until golden brown
    • smaller stars will burn much slower, for billions of years
  • Leave to cool for ~10 million years, until its core collapses and creates a supernova
